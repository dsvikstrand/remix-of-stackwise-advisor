# Italian Sausage Chowder (Shellfish-Friendly Option)

## Lightning Takeaways
- This is a rich chowder variant using Italian sausage instead of shellfish.
- If shellfish is an issue, swap clam juice for chicken broth.
- Keep sausage in medium chunks for better texture payoff.
- Add hot milk after thickening begins for faster, smoother finish.

## Who This Is For / Not For
- Good fit if you want a hearty chowder-style bowl without seafood.
- Good fit if you want a cold-weather meal with easy pantry-friendly steps.
- Not ideal if you want a very light broth profile.
- Not ideal if you want a low-fat style soup.

## Start Here: Minimum Setup
- 2 lb Italian sausage
- Butter
- 1.5 cups diced celery + white onion
- Seasoning blend: rosemary, white pepper, chicken bouillon, onion powder
- 1 cup flour
- 3 cups clam juice (or chicken broth for shellfish allergy)
- Hot milk
- Pot + spoon + measuring cups

## Playbook Steps

1) **Brown and break sausage**  
Goal: Build the meaty base.  
Common miss: Breaking sausage too fine.  
Success signal: Medium chunks remain visible.

2) **Cook aromatics with fat**  
Goal: Build sweetness/body.  
Common miss: Undercooking onion/celery.  
Success signal: Aromatics soften and smell savory.

3) **Season the base early**  
Goal: Layer flavor before liquid stage.  
Common miss: Delaying seasoning until the end.  
Success signal: Base tastes rounded before thickening.

4) **Add flour and mix through**  
Goal: Build chowder body.  
Common miss: Dry flour pockets.  
Success signal: No visible clumps.

5) **Add broth base and simmer**  
Goal: Start thickening phase.  
Common miss: Ignoring allergy substitution needs.  
Success signal: Noticeable tightening as it cooks.

6) **Add hot milk**  
Goal: Finish creamy texture efficiently.  
Common miss: Adding cold milk and slowing texture development.  
Success signal: Creamy consistency forms quickly and evenly.

7) **Return sausage and finish**  
Goal: Final texture and flavor integration.  
Common miss: Overcooking final stage.  
Success signal: Rich chowder with visible sausage texture.

## Fast Fallbacks
- Too thick: add a splash of hot liquid.
- Too thin: simmer longer, stir frequently.
- Missing clam base: chicken broth works; bump seasoning slightly.

## Red Flags
- Sausage texture becomes tiny crumble throughout.
- Raw flour taste remains.
- Thick texture but flat flavor profile.

## One Action to Take
Cook one full pot with medium sausage chunks and hot-milk finish, then tune seasoning on run two.
