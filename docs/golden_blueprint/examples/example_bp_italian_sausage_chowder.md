# Italian Sausage Chowder (Shellfish-Friendly Option)

## Lightning Takeaways
- This is a rich chowder variant using Italian sausage instead of shellfish.
- If shellfish is an issue, swap clam juice for chicken broth.
- Keep sausage in medium chunks for better texture payoff.
- Add hot milk after thickening begins for faster, smoother finish.

![Video thumbnail](https://i.ytimg.com/vi/Fjng4WHkJ6E/hqdefault.jpg)

## Summary
Build this as a cold-weather comfort chowder with strong texture and easy substitution control. Start by browning sausage in medium chunks and keep those chunks intact through the cook. That single choice carries a lot of the final “payoff” in each bowl.

Layer flavor early: cook celery/onion in fat, season the base before liquid, then work flour through fully so you do not carry raw pockets into the simmer. Once the broth goes in, watch thickness and stir consistently. The texture should tighten gradually, not jump all at once.

If shellfish is a concern, swap clam juice for chicken broth and compensate with a slightly stronger seasoning pass. Add hot milk near the end to finish smooth and creamy without slowing the thickening phase.

Use this quick operating checklist:
- Keep sausage chunky, not fine crumble.
- Fully cook aromatics before flour/liquid.
- Eliminate dry flour clumps before simmering.
- Use hot milk for finish, then stop before overcooking.

Fast fixes:
- Too thick: add hot liquid in small splashes.
- Too thin: simmer longer and stir.
- Flavor flat: adjust seasoning in small increments after texture is right.

## Playbook Steps
1) Brown sausage in medium chunks to build the base texture.
2) Sweat onion/celery fully before adding flour and liquids.
3) Season early, then add flour and remove clumps before simmer.
4) Simmer until body forms, then finish with hot milk.
5) Taste-adjust at the end for salt/seasoning balance.

## Fast Fallbacks
- Too thick: add a small splash of hot broth or milk.
- Too thin: simmer longer and stir more frequently.
- Missing clam juice: use chicken broth and slightly increase seasoning.

## Red Flags
- Sausage crumbles too fine and loses texture.
- Raw flour taste persists after simmer.
- Soup thickens but tastes flat.

## Bottom Line
Keep the sausage texture, build flavor in layers, and finish with hot milk for the fastest path to a rich chowder.
